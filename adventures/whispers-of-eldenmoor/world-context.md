# Whispers of Eldenmoor

Eldenmoor is a small village at the edge of the Ashwood, a forest that locals do not enter after dark. The harvest has failed two seasons running. Travellers say the trees themselves whisper warnings in a language nobody alive remembers.

For weeks now, livestock has gone missing. Patrols have not returned. Last night, the smith's son was taken from his own bed, and the only sign of him was a dropped wooden toy and a goblin's tooth left on the pillow.

The villagers gather in **The Broken Hearth Tavern**, the only place still warm enough to plan. Mira, the innkeeper, has been pleading with anyone who will listen — someone has to enter the Ashwood and end whatever is hunting them.

The forest holds older things than goblins. But goblins are what the village fears tonight.

## Conventions for the narrator

- The world is bleak but not nihilistic. There is hope, but it costs.
- Goblins in this world are vicious but cowardly: they ambush, they goad, they break and run when wounded badly.
- Magic is rare and old. Common villagers fear it. A Mage character should expect suspicion from NPCs, never outright hostility.
- Never describe the forest as fully safe, even after the goblin chief falls.
